Anxiety

Anxiety is a feeling of unease, such as worry or fear, that can be mild or severe. It is a normal human emotion, but if it becomes overwhelming and interferes with daily activities, it may be a sign of an anxiety disorder.

Loneliness

Loneliness is a feeling of sadness or distress because of a lack of social interaction or companionship. It can be a temporary feeling or a chronic condition that may lead to depression or other mental health problems.

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition characterized by extreme mood swings that range from mania (an elevated or irritable mood) to depression (a low mood). It can be a lifelong condition that requires ongoing treatment.

OCD

Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is a mental health condition that causes unwanted, repetitive thoughts (obsessions) and behaviors (compulsions) that can interfere with daily life. It can be treated with therapy and medication.

PTSD

Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) is a mental health condition that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event, such as combat, sexual assault, or a natural disaster. Symptoms can include flashbacks, nightmares, and anxiety.
